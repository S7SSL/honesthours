Best Chrome Extensions for Time Tracking in 2026

Chrome extensions are the most frictionless way to track time for people who work primarily in a browser. They live in your toolbar, require no switching between apps, and start recording with a single click. Here are the best options in 2026 for freelancers and small business teams.

Why a Chrome Extension for Time Tracking?

If you work in Chrome most of the day — in Gmail, Google Docs, Notion, Trello, or any web-based tool — a browser extension lets you track time without leaving your workflow. There's no separate app to open, no context switching, and no forgetting to log time because you were already in the browser.

2. Toggl Track

Best for: Freelancers tracking billable hours with clients

Toggl Track has a polished Chrome extension with idle detection (it notices when you stop working), project tracking, and team dashboards on paid plans. The free tier covers individual use well.

  • Free for 1 user
  • Paid plans from $10/user/month
  • Integrates with popular project management tools

3. Clockify

Best for: Teams that need unlimited free users

Clockify offers unlimited users on its free plan — unusual in this market. The Chrome extension lets you start timers directly from project management tools like Asana, Trello, and Jira.

  • Free for unlimited users
  • Paid plans for advanced features
  • Good for agencies and larger small businesses

4. Harvest

Best for: Freelancers who invoice clients and track expenses

Harvest combines time tracking with invoicing, so you can turn tracked hours directly into client invoices. The Chrome extension starts timers from within other tools.

  • Free for 1 user, 2 projects
  • Paid plans from $12/user/month
  • Invoice generation built in

5. RescueTime

Best for: Productivity analysis rather than billing

RescueTime runs passively in the background and analyses where you spend your time, categorising apps and websites automatically. Less useful for billing clients, more useful for understanding your work patterns.

  • Free tier available
  • Paid plans add features like focus sessions

Does Toggl have a Chrome extension?

Yes. Toggl Track has a Chrome extension that integrates with many project management tools. The free plan covers individual users.

Can I track time across multiple projects with a Chrome extension?

Yes. Most time tracking extensions including HonestHours, Toggl, and Clockify allow you to label time by project or client and switch between them.

Are browser extension time trackers accurate?

They are as accurate as the user's consistency in starting and stopping the timer. Extensions with idle detection (like Toggl) can help by prompting you when you've been inactive.
